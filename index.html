Yuki Kawamura

5'8 rookie from Japan

Here's a time line of Yuki's life:

  • 2001- Born in Yanai, Yamaguchi, Japan
  • 2007- Started playing basketball
  • 2011 (4th Grade)- Began practicing "600 shots a day" after experiencing a loss
  • 2013 (6th Grade)- Won the national championship by one point
  • 2017 (Junior High Graduation)- Graduated from Yanai Junior High School (reached the top 16 in the national middle school championship)
    Entered Fukuoka Daiichi High School
  • 2019 (3rd Year of High School)- Won four national tournament titles
  • 2020- February 28: Finished his term as a specially designated player for Yokohama
  • 2020-21 Season: Played 16 games, averaging 6.0 points, 3.4 assists, and earned the Best Five Rookie Award
  • 2022- March 3: Withdrew from Tokai University, signed a professional contract with the Yokohama B-Corsairs for the 2022-23 season
  • 2023- B.League: Named MVP, Best Five, and Rookie of the Year
  • 2024- September 6: Signed an Exhibit 10 contract with the Memphis Grizzlies in the NBA
    October 19: Agreed to a two-way contract with the Grizzlies
    October 26: Made his NBA debut against the Houston Rockets, becoming the 4th Japanese NBA player

"Each time you fail, it's important to think carefully, reflect, and take action to improve."

---Yuki Kawamura
